01 — The book

About the game

Tinea is a 2D narrative pixel art game developed with the Godot Engine, focused on exploration, environmental storytelling, puzzles, and subtle psychological horror.

The story follows a protagonist who returns to her family’s old library after the death of her mother. While organizing the place, she finds objects left behind by people who passed through the library over the years.

Each item carries a small story: a photograph, a note, a forgotten book, a pressed flower, a hurried annotation. Little by little, these traces reveal not only her mother’s life, but also the collective memory of a community.

internal file

Tinea is less about solving a death and more about understanding what remains after someone is gone.

03 — The archive

Objects that tell stories

In Tinea, collectibles are not there only to be found. Each object is a memory, a fragment of someone, a small mark left inside the library.

📷

Photographs

Old photos with handwritten notes on the back. Some capture simple moments. Others seem to hold something left unsaid.

📚

Books

Donated, borrowed, or forgotten books. Some carry dedications, markings, folded pages, and small traces of those who read them.

🎟

Bookmarks

Tickets, pressed flowers, notes, shopping lists, and small pieces of paper used to mark pages.

📝

Mother’s notes

Records of everyday events written by Maria de Fátima, the former librarian.

🖼

Paintings

Paintings made by children, portraits, old phrases, and images that reveal the library’s connection with the community.

☕

Small traces

A forgotten coat, a recipe, a bingo card, a plush cat. Small things that remain.

04 — Atmosphere

Rain, silence, and old paper

Tinea aims for a melancholic and contemplative atmosphere, where the player has time to observe the details of the environment. The game exists between silence, rain, old wood, and the small noises of an almost empty library.

07 — Inspirations

Games, feelings, and references

Tinea comes from a mix of narrative games, contemplative indie projects, melancholic atmospheres, psychological horror, and stories that use space itself as a way to say what dialogue alone cannot.

Sally Face

For its mystery, strangeness, and the feeling of a childhood marked by something wrong.

Fran Bow

For its psychological horror, dark fantasy, and emotional fragility.

Little Misfortune

For its contrast between cuteness, humor, tragedy, and discomfort.

Enigma do Medo

For its investigation, Brazilian atmosphere, and mystery around memory and absence.

Coffee Talk

For its cozy feeling, intimate conversations, and rainy night mood.

Milk Inside / Outside

For its fragmented, subjective, and uncomfortable narrative.

GRIS

For its visual and sensitive way of portraying pain, loss, and reconstruction.

OMORI / To the Moon

For their relationship between memory, affection, pain, and what remains hidden.
